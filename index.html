ABOUT

Curd rice, also called yogurt rice, is a dish originating from India. The word "curd" in Indian English refers to unsweetened probiotic yogurt. It is most popular in the South Indian states of Tamil Nadu, Kerala , Karnataka, Telangana and Andhra Pradesh; and also in West Indian states of Rajasthan, Gujarat and Maharashtra.In the state of Rajasthan it is known as Auliya,in Gujarat it is known as Ghens and in Maharashtra it is known as dahi bhat. In the state of Tamil Nadu it is called thayir saadam or soru, in Kerala it is called tayire ch≈çre and in Telangana and Andhra Pradesh it is called perugannam/daddojanam. In Karnataka, it is called mosaranna. The dish is a staple of traditional cuisine, with the untempered version present at the end of almost every Indian meal during summers. In the states of Rajasthan and Gujarat, curd rice varieties Auliya and Ghens are prepared on Shitala Saptami and Randhan Chhath festival. The tempered version is often served during formal occasions and also offered as prasadam (blessed food) to devotees in temples.

VARIETIES

BEETROOT CURD RICE

Beetroot curd rice is a colorful and nutritious recipe that is perfect for a wholesome meal. To prepare this dish, cook the rice until it is fluffy and set aside. Temper mustard seeds, urad dal, and chopped green chilies in a pan lined with ghee. Add grated beetroot, a pinch of hing, and some curd to the mixture and cook until fragrant.

TOMATO CURD RICE

Tomato curd rice is a tangy and flavorful recipe that is perfect for a quick meal. To prepare this dish, cook the rice until it is fluffy and set aside. Temper mustard seeds, jeera, and split green chilies in a pan lined with ghee. Add chopped onions and chopped tomatoes to the mixture and cook until the tomatoes are soft.

PEANUT CURD RICE

Peanut curd rice is a crunchy and nutty recipe that is perfect for a satisfying meal. To prepare this dish, cook the rice until it is fluffy and set aside. Temper mustard seeds, urad dal, and chopped green chilies in a pan lined with peanut oil. Add roasted and crushed peanuts, grated coconut, and curry leaves to the mixture and cook until fragrant
